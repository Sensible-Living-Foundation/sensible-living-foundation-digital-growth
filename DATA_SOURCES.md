# Data Sources

## Purpose

This file lists recommended public data sources for planning Sensible Living Foundation programs, especially:

- Sense Gardens target areas
- Financial Sense target areas
- future community impact mapping and dashboards

These sources should be used when building maps, dashboards, and planning tools in this repository.

---

# Core Mapping and Boundary Data

## U.S. Census Bureau - TIGER/Line Shapefiles
Use for:
- census tract boundaries
- geographic mapping layers
- linking ACS data to maps

Recommended for:
- Sense Gardens opportunity maps
- Financial Sense target area maps

Official source:
https://www.census.gov/geographies/mapping-files/time-series/geo/tiger-line-file.html

---

# Community Demographic and Economic Data

## U.S. Census Bureau - American Community Survey (ACS)
Use for:
- poverty
- household income
- educational attainment
- housing data
- other community conditions

Recommended for:
- identifying low-income or higher-need areas
- comparing neighborhoods and census tracts
- supporting grant and planning narratives

Official sources:
https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs.html
https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs/data.html

---

# Sense Gardens Data Sources

## USDA Food Access Research Atlas
Use for:
- identifying low-access areas
- identifying low-income and low-access census tracts
- food desert-related planning

Recommended for:
- Sense Gardens opportunity mapping
- food access justification

Official source:
https://www.ers.usda.gov/data-products/food-access-research-atlas

## CDC / ATSDR Social Vulnerability Index (SVI)
Use for:
- identifying communities with higher vulnerability
- adding a broader community risk layer

Recommended for:
- prioritizing communities that may benefit from added support
- strengthening community need analysis

Official source:
https://www.atsdr.cdc.gov/place-health/php/svi/index.html

## Arizona / Local Community Site Data
Use for:
- community centers
- schools
- parks
- public facilities
- possible garden host sites

Potential official local source:
City of Phoenix open data and GIS resources

Example source:
https://maps.phoenix.gov/pub/rest/services/Public/ParksOpenData/MapServer/1

# Financial Sense Data Sources

## Arizona Department of Education - Title I
Use for:
- identifying Title I schools and districts
- targeting school-based financial literacy opportunities

Recommended for:
- school placement strategy
- district targeting
- identifying communities with higher economic need

Official sources:
https://www.azed.gov/titlei
https://www.azed.gov/data/public-data-sets

## NCES Common Core of Data (CCD)
Use for:
- school and district reference data
- school names
- addresses
- geocodes
- public school and district data

Recommended for:
- validating school locations
- creating school layers for maps
- matching schools and districts across datasets

Official sources:
https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/
https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/files.asp
https://nces.ed.gov/programs/edge/geographic/schoollocations

## U.S. Census Bureau - ACS
Use for:
- tract-level income and poverty analysis
- selecting neighborhoods for community-based financial literacy programming

Official sources:
https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs.html
https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs/data.html

# Supporting Financial Capability and Well-Being Sources

## FINRA Foundation - National Financial Capability Study (NFCS)
Use for:
- broader financial capability context
- state-level or broad-market financial literacy need statements
- research support for proposals

Recommended for:
- grant narratives
- program rationale
- supporting research

Official sources:
https://www.finrafoundation.org/national-financial-capability-study
https://finrafoundation.org/nfcs-data-and-downloads

## CFPB - Financial Well-Being Survey Data
Use for:
- financial well-being context
- research support
- framing program outcomes around financial well-being

Recommended for:
- supporting narratives
- evaluation planning
- measurement ideas for future Financial Sense outcomes

Official sources:
https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/financial-well-being-survey-data/
https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/educator-tools/financial-well-being-resources/measure-and-score/

# Notes for Contributors
