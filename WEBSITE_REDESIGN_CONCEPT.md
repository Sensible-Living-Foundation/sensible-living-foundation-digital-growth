# Sensible Living Foundation Website Redesign Concept

## Purpose

This document outlines a concept for improving the Sensible Living Foundation website.

The goal is to create a website that clearly communicates the mission, encourages community engagement, and helps visitors take action through donations, volunteering, and partnerships.

The redesign concept should guide contributors who are proposing improvements to the website.

---

# Current Website

Current website:

https://www.sensiblelivingfoundation.org

The site currently serves as an informational platform that introduces the foundation and its mission.

Future improvements should focus on improving clarity, storytelling, engagement, and supporter conversion.

---

# Redesign Goals

The redesigned website should help visitors:

• understand the mission quickly  
• learn about programs  
• donate easily  
• volunteer or partner  
• stay connected through email updates  

The website should be:

• simple  
• mission-driven  
• mobile friendly  
• easy to navigate  
• optimized for search engines  

---

# Recommended Website Structure

## Homepage

The homepage should clearly introduce the foundation and guide visitors toward meaningful action.

Suggested homepage sections:

1. Mission Hero Section  
2. Wealth and Health Pillars  
3. Featured Programs  
4. Why This Work Matters  
5. Community Impact Vision  
6. Ways to Get Involved  
7. Newsletter Signup  
8. Footer

---

## Mission Hero Section

The hero section should quickly explain the foundation purpose.

Example message:

Helping communities build stronger futures through financial literacy, healthy living, and community-driven solutions.

The hero section should include:

• mission statement  
• supporting image or visual  
• call-to-action buttons

Examples of calls to action:

Donate  
Volunteer  
Partner With Us

---

## Wealth and Health Pillars

The homepage should introduce the two major pillars of the foundation:

### Wealth

Programs that improve financial knowledge and economic opportunity.

Examples:

• financial literacy education  
• financial empowerment workshops  
• youth financial education

### Health

Programs that improve community health and access to healthy living.

Examples:

• Sense Gardens  
• nutrition education  
• community wellness initiatives

---

## Featured Programs

The homepage should highlight the main programs.

Programs may include:

• Financial Literacy Education  
• Sense Gardens  
• Community Health Initiatives

Each program section should include:

• short description  
• image  
• link to learn more

---

## Why This Work Matters

This section should explain the problems the foundation addresses.

Examples:

• lack of financial education  
• limited access to healthy food  
• underserved community resources

This helps visitors understand the importance of the mission.

---

## Community Impact Vision

Since the foundation is still growing, this section may focus on vision and goals.

Examples:

• communities served  
• gardens developed  
• people educated  
• partnerships built

Future versions may include measurable impact metrics.

---

## Ways to Get Involved

This section should guide visitors toward action.

Possible actions:

• Donate  
• Volunteer  
• Partner with the foundation  
• Join the newsletter

Clear call-to-action buttons should be included.

---

## Newsletter Signup

Encourage visitors to stay connected.

Simple form fields:

• name  
• email

Visitors who sign up should receive updates about:

• foundation progress  
• upcoming programs  
• volunteer opportunities  
• impact stories

---

# Program Pages

Each program should have its own page.

Example structure:

## Program Overview

What the program is and why it matters.

## Community Benefit

How the program helps people.

## How to Participate

Volunteer opportunities or participation options.

## Support the Program

Donation or sponsorship options.

---

# Donation Page

The donation page should clearly communicate:

• why donations matter  
• how funds support programs  
• opportunities for recurring giving  

It should include:

• secure donation option  
• suggested donation amounts  
• message of appreciation

---

# Volunteer Page

The volunteer page should make it easy for people to help.

Volunteer form fields:

• name  
• email  
• phone  
• interests  
• availability

Volunteer opportunities may include:

• garden support  
• financial literacy programs  
• event support  
• marketing or outreach

---

# Corporate Partnership Page

Businesses and organizations should be able to partner with the foundation.

This page should include:

• overview of the mission  
• examples of partnership opportunities  
• employee volunteer programs  
• sponsorship opportunities  
• partnership contact form

---

# SEO and Discoverability

The redesigned website should improve search visibility.

Focus areas:

• page titles  
• meta descriptions  
• keyword targeting  
• internal linking  
• image alt text

Example keyword areas:

• financial literacy nonprofit  
• community garden nonprofit  
• nutrition education nonprofit  
• community health programs

---

# Design Principles

The website design should prioritize:

• simplicity  
• clarity  
• storytelling  
• accessibility  
• mobile usability

---

# Contributor Role

Contributors can help by:

• suggesting improved layouts  
• proposing content improvements  
• recommending SEO strategies  
• improving supporter engagement flows

All suggestions should align with the mission and remain practical for a nonprofit organization.
